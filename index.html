Contextual-POV: Evaluating Situated Spatial Reasoning in Vision-Language Models

Mimo Shirasaka1 Haochen Zhang2 Yonatan Bisk2
1The University of Tokyo 2Carnegie Mellon University

TL;DR We introduce POVBench, which disentangles three forms of observer grounding: given, stated, and inferred. Evaluation reveals that SOTA VLMs struggle most when the viewpoint must be inferred, and we explore reasoning methods that improve localization.

Abstract

Reasoning over language instructions in embodied tasks such as robotics often requires 3D understanding. Humans resolve implicit reference to objects through shared context, perspective-taking, and commonsense. Recent vision-language models (VLMs) appear capable of spatial reasoning in 2D, yet fail at embodied perspective reasoning: inferring an object's location from an implicit reference by combining perspective-taking with commonsense knowledge about human activity and physical environments. To address this, we construct the Point-of-View Benchmark (POVBench), a dataset of 3D scenes and queries that disentangles three forms of observer grounding in natural embodied communication: given, stated, and inferred. The tasks require models to reason over multi-view observations and localize target objects from situated spatial and contextual cues. Across state-of-the-art VLMs, we find that models struggle to map both implicit and explicit directional language into accurate object localizations, especially when the observer's perspective must be inferred from context. We further show that explicit reasoning over observer-centered spatial relations improves localization of underspecified objects.
